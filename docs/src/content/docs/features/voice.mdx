title: Voice Memos
description: Send voice notes and get them transcribed automatically.
---

Clawbolt supports voice messages. Send a voice note via Telegram or an audio message via iMessage/RCS and it gets transcribed and processed as if you typed the message.

## How it works

1. **Record a voice note**: Use Telegram's voice message feature or send an audio message from iMessage/RCS
2. **Transcription**: The audio is processed by [faster-whisper](https://github.com/SYSTRAN/faster-whisper), a fast and accurate speech-to-text engine
3. **Processing**: The transcribed text is fed into the agent loop just like a typed message
4. **Response**: Clawbolt responds to the content of your voice note

## When to use voice

Voice memos are great for:
- **On the job site**: When your hands are dirty or you're holding tools
- **Driving between jobs**: Quickly capture thoughts or requests
- **Detailed descriptions**: When typing would take too long
- **Job descriptions**: Describe the job verbally and Clawbolt processes the details

## Phone dictation

You can also use your phone's built-in dictation (the microphone button on your keyboard) to compose messages. Your phone converts your speech to text locally, then sends the text to Clawbolt like any other message. This works on any channel and doesn't require any server-side setup.

Dictation is great for quick messages when you don't need a full voice memo. Clawbolt handles typos and rough sentences well, so don't worry about perfect transcription.

## Requirements

Voice transcription requires `ffmpeg` for audio processing. The Docker image includes ffmpeg by default. For local development, install it via your system package manager.
